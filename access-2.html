Improving Design

Sip #2: CAPTCHAs

CAPTCHAs help protect a website from spam by separating human users from computers. However, CAPTCHAs are not always accessible for users. CAPTCHAs that rely on visuals like selecting the squares with the traffic lights are only solvable for visual users. Users with cognitive disabilities could also have difficulty with visual based CAPTCHAs like deciphering distorted text. CAPTCHAs that are difficult to interact with can lead to user frustration and make them more likely to leave a site.

CAPTCHAs can be tricky to balance. To learn more about making CAPTCHAs accessible to everyone, consult this resource from The Bureau of Internet Accessibility.
